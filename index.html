  • Source of energy: Solar irradiance incident on rooftop PV modules (monocrystalline preferred for higher efficiency). System sized to ~5 kW peak (STC) to meet daytime load and enable grid export.
  • Conversion process: PV modules generate DC ‚Üí MPPT controller optimizes voltage/current ‚Üí inverter converts to synchronized AC for loads or grid. Batteries (Li-ion) store surplus power; charge controller and BMS manage storage and protection.

  • Design notes: Proper string sizing (Voc/Isc), shading analysis, earthing, lightning protection, and inverter compatibility must be verified for reliability and efficiency.

  • Conversion process: Warm seawater heats a working fluid (e.g., ammonia) in an evaporator ‚Üí vapor drives turbine-generator ‚Üí cold deep seawater condenses vapor, repeating cycle. Closed-cycle OTEC produces electricity continuously.
